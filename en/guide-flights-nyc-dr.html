JFK, Newark or LaGuardia: Which Airport for Your DR Flight?

All three New York airports fly to the Dominican Republic — but not the same way. Here's the real comparison so you don't waste time on 20 browser tabs.

Quick Comparison

AirportDirect AirlinesApprox. Flight Time
JFK (Queens)JetBlue, American, Delta~3h 50min
Newark — EWR (NJ)United, JetBlue~3h 55min
LaGuardia — LGA (Queens)Delta, American (select routes)~3h 50min

JFK typically has the most direct flights per week — JetBlue alone runs dozens of weekly flights to Punta Cana and Santo Domingo. If you live in Manhattan, Brooklyn, or the Bronx, JFK is usually the most practical. If you're in New Jersey, Newark saves you the tunnel or bridge crossing.

When Each Airport Makes Sense

JFK: Best overall selection — most schedule options and direct flights to the most DR cities (Punta Cana, Santo Domingo, Santiago, Puerto Plata). Start here.

Newark: Best if you live in NJ or the western edge of NYC. United has solid options. JetBlue also flies out of EWR.

LaGuardia: Fewer direct DR flights, but occasionally cheaper fares on specific dates. Always worth comparing before dismissing.

Money-saving tip: Search all three airports at the same time. A $40 price difference doesn't justify choosing the "convenient" airport — check all three first, then decide.

How to Find the Cheapest Fares

Book 6–8 weeks in advance for the best prices — not last minute. Set a price alert on Google Flights or Hopper for your specific route. Fares tend to be lower on Tuesday and Wednesday searches. Avoid booking during school breaks, Thanksgiving, Christmas, and Semana Santa — those weeks are peak pricing.

What Nobody Explains About Baggage

Most airlines flying to the DR charge for checked bags — it's NOT included in the basic fare. If you're traveling as a family, it's often cheaper to add bags at booking time than to pay at the airport. Check this before you confirm your purchase — the difference can be $30–50 per bag.
