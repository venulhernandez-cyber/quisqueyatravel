Dominican Republic Entry Requirements 2026

Check this before you buy your ticket — passport rules, the free E-Ticket form, and what nobody tells you at the airport.

Before you book anything, make sure your documents are in order. Getting this wrong means stress at the check-in counter — or worse, getting denied boarding. Here's everything you need, explained plainly.

The Non-Negotiables

  • Valid passport with at least 6 months remaining before it expires. If yours is close to expiring, renew it now — processing times vary by country and can take several weeks.
  • E-Ticket (online entry form) — the Dominican Republic requires all travelers to complete this form before arrival. It's free, takes 5 minutes, and is done online at the official government site.
  • Return or onward ticket — airlines and immigration require proof that you're leaving. Have it printed or on your phone.
About the E-Ticket: Fill it out within 72 hours before your departure. You'll receive a QR code — save it offline or print it. Immigration scans it on arrival.

Do US Citizens Need a Visa?

No. US citizens, Canadian citizens, UK citizens, and most EU nationals do not need a visa to enter the Dominican Republic for tourism stays up to 30 days. You enter on your valid passport and the E-Ticket form alone.

⚠️ Born in the DR with US citizenship? You still enter on your US passport. No visa needed. If you also have a Dominican cédula (ID card), bring it — it can be useful for banking or family matters while you're there, but it's not required for entry.

Traveling with Children

If a child is traveling with only one parent, some airlines may request a notarized letter of consent from the other parent authorizing the travel. This is not always required, but it's better to have it and not need it. Check with your specific airline before the trip.

Travel Insurance

Travel insurance is not mandatory for the Dominican Republic, but it's worth considering — especially if you're traveling during hurricane season (June–November), or with elderly family members. A basic policy covers trip cancellations and emergency medical costs.

Quick Pre-Travel Checklist

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months beyond travel dates
  • E-Ticket completed within 72 hours of departure
  • Return/onward ticket confirmed
  • If traveling with a minor and only one parent: notarized consent letter
  • Travel insurance (optional but recommended Jun–Nov)
