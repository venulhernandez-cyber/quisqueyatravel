How Much Does a Family Trip to the DR Cost?

The number everyone wants but nobody gives you straight. Here it is — real costs, no fluff, so you know exactly what to save before booking.

This is the question I get asked most. So I'll answer it with actual numbers — not "it depends" — so you can build a real budget before you start shopping for flights.

Budget Breakdown for a Family of 4 — 7 Nights

These numbers are for a regular travel week (not Christmas, not Easter/Semana Santa, when everything spikes). Use this as your planning baseline.

ExpenseEstimated Cost
Flights (4 people, round-trip from JFK/EWR/LGA)$1,200–$1,800
Hotel (7 nights, family room or 2 doubles)$560–$1,050
Food (if not all-inclusive)$350–$700
Local transport (taxi, bus, car rental)$150–$350
Activities & excursions$200–$500
Total Estimate$2,460–$4,400

How to Lower That Number Without Ruining the Trip

Travel in low season (May, June, September) — flights and hotels drop significantly without the beach getting any worse. Look at all-inclusive resorts: even if the room costs more, you save on food and transport because it's all included. Book flights 6–8 weeks ahead, not last minute.

All-inclusive math: A family of 4 at a mid-range all-inclusive might pay $120–160/person/night — but that includes all meals, drinks, beach access, and most activities. Compare that to paying separately and the math often works in favor of all-inclusive for families.

What Almost Nobody Budgets For

Travel insurance, tips at the all-inclusive (yes, it's expected), and the gifts you inevitably end up buying for family back home. Leave a buffer of $150–$200 extra per trip — it always shows up.

The Bottom Line

A family of 4 traveling 7 nights to a mid-range all-inclusive, including flights, should budget between $3,500 and $5,500 total. That's not the cheapest Caribbean vacation — but the Dominican Republic gives you more beach, better food culture, and lower prices than comparable destinations like Jamaica, Barbados, or Turks and Caicos.
